Keep Building
Disputes are just one lever. Here are three more ways to move your score.
Lever 1 — Done
Remove Negative Items
You just did this. Your dispute letters challenge collections, late payments, and inaccurate info on your report. Once bureaus delete unverifiable items, your score starts recovering immediately. This alone can move you 30-100+ points.
Lever 2 — Start Now
Add Positive Tradelines
Every open account that reports "paid as agreed" adds a positive tradeline to your file. More positive history = higher score. Three ways to do this:
Secured credit card — Put down a $200 deposit, get a $200 credit line. Use it for one small recurring bill (streaming, phone). Pay the full balance every month. After 6-12 months you'll graduate to an unsecured card and get your deposit back.
Credit builder loan — You make monthly payments into a locked savings account. Once the loan is paid off, you get the money. Every payment reports to all 3 bureaus as an installment loan paid on time.
Authorized user — If a family member with good credit adds you to their card, their payment history can appear on your report. No risk to them if you never use the card.

Lever 3 — Quick Win
Fix Your Utilization
Credit utilization is 30% of your score. It measures how much of your available credit you're using. Under 30% is good. Under 10% is ideal. This updates every billing cycle — it's the fastest score lever you have.
Pay down balances — Get every card below 30% of its limit. If your limit is $1,000, keep the balance under $300.
Pay before statement closes — Make a payment a few days before your statement date. The lower balance is what gets reported.
Request a limit increase — Higher limit, same spending = lower utilization. Call your card issuer and ask. Most will do a soft pull.
Balance transfer — Move high-interest debt to a 0% APR card. Consolidates payments and can lower per-card utilization.
Starting From Zero?
The One-Card Strategy
If you have no credit history or you're rebuilding from scratch, you only need one card and one bill. Here's the play:
1. Open a secured card (Capital One, Discover, or Chime above)
2. Put one small recurring bill on it — your phone, a streaming service, anything under $30
3. Set up autopay to pay the full balance every month
4. Don't use the card for anything else
5. Wait 6 months. You'll have a score and positive history.
That's it. One card, one bill, autopay. In 6-12 months you'll have enough history to qualify for better cards, auto loans, and apartments. The secured deposit comes back when you upgrade.
More available credit is not a bad thing when managed correctly. A common myth is that opening a new card hurts your score. The hard inquiry is a small, temporary dip (5-10 points for a few months). But the new tradeline and increased available credit actually help your score long-term by lowering utilization and adding positive payment history. The key is discipline: get the card, use it for one bill, pay it every month, and leave it alone. That's how people go from no score to 700+ in under a year.
