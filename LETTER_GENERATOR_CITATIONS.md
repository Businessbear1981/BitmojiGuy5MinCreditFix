# Letter Generator — Citations & Legal References

## Overview

The letter generator creates professional dispute letters citing **FCRA** (Fair Credit Reporting Act) and **FDCPA** (Fair Debt Collection Practices Act) statutes. Each letter includes specific legal citations and state-specific laws.

## Dispute Type Templates & Citations

### **1. Collections Dispute**

**Variants:**
- **A: Debt Validation Demand**
  - Cites: FDCPA § 809(b), FCRA § 611
  - Demands: Original creditor info, account number, payment history, signed agreement
  - Timeline: 30 days

- **B: Collection Statute of Limitations Challenge**
  - Cites: FCRA § 605 (7-year rule)
  - Challenges: Date of first delinquency, re-aging violations
  - Demands: Verification within 30 days or deletion

- **C: Cease Collection and Remove**
  - Cites: FCRA § 611(a)(5)(A)
  - Demands: Immediate investigation, deletion if unverifiable
  - Escalation: CFPB complaint threat

---

### **2. Late Payments Dispute**

**Variants:**
- **A: Goodwill Late Payment Removal**
  - Approach: Goodwill gesture request
  - Rationale: Temporary hardship, now resolved, account current
  - Emotional appeal: Housing/financing qualification

- **B: Late Payment Accuracy Dispute**
  - Cites: FCRA § 611
  - Challenges: Payment date accuracy, posting delays
  - Demands: Furnisher verification, proof of posting date

---

### **3. Wrong Addresses Dispute**

**Variants:**
- **A: Mixed File Correction**
  - Cites: FCRA § 607(b) (maximum possible accuracy)
  - Challenges: Incorrect address on account
  - Demands: Correction of address, verification of accounts

- **B: Address Fraud Prevention**
  - Cites: FCRA § 604 (permissible purposes)
  - Challenges: Unauthorized address changes
  - Demands: Address correction, fraud investigation

---

### **4. Unknown Accounts Dispute**

**Variants:**
- **A: Identity Theft Blocking**
  - Cites: FCRA § 605B
  - Demands: Account blocking, fraud investigation
  - Escalation: Identity theft report filed

- **B: Unrecognized Account Removal**
  - Cites: FCRA § 611
  - Challenges: Account not opened by consumer
  - Demands: Deletion if unverifiable

---

### **5. Aged Debt Dispute**

**Variants:**
- **A: Statute of Limitations Enforcement**
  - Cites: FCRA § 605 (7-year rule)
  - Challenges: Account exceeds reporting period
  - Demands: Immediate deletion

- **B: Re-aging Violation Challenge**
  - Cites: FCRA § 605(c)
  - Challenges: Date of first delinquency cannot change
  - Demands: Verification of original delinquency date

---

### **6. Method of Verification (MOV) Demand**

**Variants:**
- **A: MOV Demand (Initial)**
  - Cites: FCRA § 611(a)(6)(B)(iii)
  - Demands: Description of verification procedure, business name/address/phone of furnisher
  - References: Court cases on reasonable reinvestigation

- **B: MOV Demand (Final)**
  - Cites: FCRA § 611(a)(6)(B)(iii)
  - Challenges: Automated e-OSCAR system without genuine reinvestigation
  - Case law: Cushman v. Trans Union Corp., Johnson v. MBNA America Bank

## Escalation Language

### **30-Day Reinvestigation Deadline**
All letters reference the FCRA § 611 requirement that credit bureaus must:
1. Reinvestigate within 30 days
2. Contact the furnisher
3. Provide results to consumer
4. Delete if unverifiable

### **Deletion Demands**
Letters cite FCRA § 611(a)(5)(A):
> "If the reinvestigation does not resolve the dispute, the consumer reporting agency shall note the disputed information in the consumer's file and in any subsequent consumer report containing the information."

### **Damages & Remedies**
Final demand letters reference:
- FCRA § 616: Willful noncompliance ($100-$1,000 per violation)
- FCRA § 617: Negligent noncompliance
- Attorney fees and punitive damages
- CFPB complaint filing

## Letter Structure

Every letter follows this structure:

1. **Opening**: Formal dispute notice + statute citation
2. **Disputed Items**: List of accounts/issues
3. **Consumer Reference**: Name + confirmation number
4. **Dispute Rationale**: Specific legal grounds
5. **Demands**: Specific actions required
6. **State Law Block**: State-specific consumer protections
7. **Closing**: Signature line + date
